History of foiling

The early development of hydrofoils started over 100 years ago when Italian Enrico Forlanini achieved 36.9 knots with his 60hp airscrew-driven boat in 1906. Several engineers took notice, among them the Wright Brothers and Alexander Graham Bell, both of whom experimented with foilborne craft. What issues does foiling solve?

Foil windsurfing makes it possible to enjoy windsurfing even in locations where the wind is only blowing at 7 to 10 knots. Foiling is basically windsurfing in lighter wind conditions, which means you would have more time in the water! Foil windsurfing is also very low-risk and can be learned by everyone.

How to foil surf

It''s quite simple to understand the mechanics of foils. The process is similar to airplane or birds' wings. The more you lean back on your back foot, the more the wing will tilt upright, creating more lift. In the opposite direction, the more applied weight on your front foot, the more the wing will dive underwater. Finding your balance is key to controlling the hydrofoil surfboard. author: surfertoday.com

Who is foil surfing?

Foil Surfing just like any other type of surfing can be learned by anybody who is willing to and who possess the required equipment. In addition, since the sport can be exercised in almost any kind of weather and current, it's easily accesible to anybody at any time.

Future of foil surfing

Over the next few years, we will see more and more people discover the benefits of foil surfing and maybe one day it will be very commonplace. It will be a stepping stone into the sport for many, a great introduction to the world of watersports. I have found that those who enjoy the challenge and thrill of riding this board are in it for the full experience and will slowly get used to this new skill.
